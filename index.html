Six things I've worked on.

01 / VR Rehabilitation Honours research
USyd × Guide Dogs Australia Lead Honours Researcher · 2026

A VR supermarket for adults with post-stroke hemianopia.

My Honours project. A mixed-methods pilot looking at whether a VR-based supermarket simulation can help people regain everyday independence after stroke. I helped bring Guide Dogs Australia in as the project's first external partner.

VR supermarket simulation
02 / Pre-op explainer videos Clinical research
“

Can a short video before surgery actually make patients feel less anxious?

the question we're asking
NSW Health · Sydney LHD Research Lead · 2025 to present

Working out whether pre-op videos help patients feel less anxious before surgery.

A mixed-methods study across NSW hospitals, looking at patient anxiety, information needs, and clinical workflow. Supervised by Harkness Fellow A/Prof Sarah Aitken, who's also a vascular surgeon.

03 / ALERT lymphoedema clinic Clinical research · Data
Macquarie University Hospital Research Intern · 2024 to present

Cleaning up clinic data, then helping with a study on personalised compression therapy.

I noticed the clinic was losing time on small documentation errors, so I helped redesign the weekly data-capture process. That work led to a continuing intern offer on an upcoming clinical paper.

ICG lymphography poster
04 / Pharmacogenomics review Systematic review
Reviewing
  • Randomised controlled trials
  • Multi-gene panels (3 or more genes)
  • Primary care settings
  • Psychiatric care settings
Stanford-affiliated team Research Contributor · 2025

A systematic review on multi-gene pharmacogenomic testing in primary and psychiatric care.

Working alongside a Stanford-affiliated researcher on the meta-analysis. The kind of evidence work that takes a long time but matters when it lands.

05 / Glimmr Healthtech · Hackathon
Harvard HealthTech Innovation Hackathon Co-founder · 2025

Glimmr. An AI eye-screening idea for catching glaucoma early in rural communities.

Pitched at Harvard. I led the clinical side of things, thinking about how something like this would actually fit into a rural patient's referral journey rather than just sit on a shelf as a demo.
